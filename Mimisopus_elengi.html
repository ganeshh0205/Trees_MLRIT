🌳 Mimusops elengi

Common Names: Spanish Cherry, Bakul, Maulsari, Bullet Wood
Family: Sapotaceae
Botanical Name: Mimusops elengi
Native To: South Asia & Southeast Asia
Lifespan: 100+ years
🌼 Did You Know?
The sweet fragrance of its flowers is so calming that it's used in spiritual rituals and Ayurvedic oils.

🌟 Why This Tree is Special

  • 🌸 Sacred and Symbolic: Revered in Indian culture and often planted near temples.
  • 💐 Heavenly Fragrance: Star-like flowers with sweet aroma used in rituals and perfumes.
  • 🌿 Medicinal Treasure: Used in Ayurveda for skin, digestion, and dental health.
  • 🌳 Evergreen Elegance: Glossy green leaves and year-round flowers make it stunning.

✅ Key Benefits

  • 🌼 Fragrant Flowers: Used in garlands, cosmetics, and ceremonies
  • 🌿 Bark & Leaves: Treat ulcers, gum issues, and skin problems
  • 🍈 Fruits: Edible and mildly sweet, used in traditional remedies
  • 🪵 Wood: Hard and durable, ideal for tools and furniture
  • 🧘 Calming Properties: Reduces stress and promotes tranquility

✨ Ayurvedic Insights:
In Ayurveda, Mimusops elengi is used as a remedy for bleeding gums, toothache, and bad breath.
🐝 Its nectar attracts honeybees, supporting biodiversity.
🧘 Meditating under a Bakul tree was believed to enhance memory and clarity.
